Theo Passingham, a visual artist and creative force, is renowned for their ability to transform live musical performances into multi-sensory experiences. Born with a natural flair for art and design, they completed their Fine Art/Photography degree at Kingston University, which became the foundation of their diverse skill set.

In the bustling music scenes of Bristol and beyond, Theo has become a pivotal figure. Their talents shone brightly when they were given just two weeks to curate the visual content for Shambala Festival's Kaleida Stage. With swift precision, they assembled a team of creatives, sourced technical equipment, and crafted bespoke visuals that resonated with the unique atmosphere of each performance. Their success led to further opportunities, enhancing their reputation as a master of live visual production.

Theo's project Tikoda showcases their versatility as a Bristol-based electronic music artist and producer, where they blend an array of genres to create deeply immersive audio-visual shows. Collaborations with a diverse range of artists, including Jausme, Tamsin Elliot, and A.Whales, have resulted in performances that are both vibrant and nuanced, earning acclaim for their ability to evoke a spectrum of emotions.

Their mastery extends into mastering music, where they apply a hands-on approach, working in partnership with clients to ensure each track is polished to perfection. Artists and labels alike praise Theo's meticulous nature, a testament to their dedication to preserving the integrity of the music while enhancing its quality.

A pioneer in the realm of spatial sound, Theo's postgraduate studies in Innovation in Sound at dBs Institute sparked a passion for creating bespoke multi-channel sound effects. Their collaboration with d&b Audiotechnik and the use of advanced systems like d&b Soundscape reflect their commitment to making high-level audio technology accessible and intuitive for artists and technicians.

Theo's influence on the alt-folk band Young Waters as a composer, agent, and manager has seen the band soar to new heights. From arranging their harmonious strings to steering their path through the music industry, Theo's role has been integral to the band's success.

Their visual work for Grove at Thekla, Bristol, is a recent highlight, where they seamlessly integrated visuals with the venue's lighting and sound. This project, along with their animated visuals for Dareshack's Soundscape launch, underlines Theo's exceptional talent for creating captivating visual narratives that complement the music and engage audiences.

In essence, Theo Passingham is a visionary artist whose technical expertise and creative instincts merge to push the boundaries of live performance, whether through the pulsating beats of a club or the serene melodies of folk music. Their continued evolution and innovation in the field make them an indispensable asset to the music and visual arts community.
